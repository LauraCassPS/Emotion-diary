About Emotions

Why we log

Emotions are not random and they are not faults. Each one is produced for a reason, and each one is trying to tell you something. This is what your diary entries are built on.

How an emotion gets made

Input

Your five senses take in information from a situation.

↓

Processing

Your brain compares it against memory and past experience.

↓

Output

An emotion is produced, and that emotion drives an action.

The same event can produce different emotions in different people, because it's being compared against different stored memories.

What each emotion is for
Happy Signals gain

Something was achieved or gained. It reinforces the behaviour that got you there, so you repeat it.

Sad Signals loss

Something was lost or damaged. Its purpose is to communicate a need and invite support, comfort, or care.

Angry Signals injustice

Something registers as unfair or wrong. It pushes you to change the situation, or to change how you're acting in it.

Anxious Signals threat

Something registers as a threat. This one is innate and inherited, produced to protect you and keep you alert. It's survival wiring, not a flaw.

Mind, body, behaviour
Mind→Body→Behaviour

Biological reaction

Automatic and innate. Fires before thought when something registers as a threat. Not a choice.

Learnt reaction

Built from thinking and past experience. This is the part that can be noticed, traced, and changed.

What the body does: muscles tense, heart rate and blood pressure rise, breathing changes. These often show up before you consciously notice the emotion, which makes them useful early signals.

Why logging works
1

Notice the pattern

Log two or three entries a day. Over time the repeats become visible, and a pattern is much easier to work with than a single bad moment.

2

Create a gap

Acting without thinking first is where most regret comes from. Writing the emotion down puts a pause between feeling it and acting on it.

3

Trace it back

Follow the reaction to the stored experience it came from. Naming the emotion accurately is the first step, which is what the Words tab is for.

4

Understand it

Much of the intensity comes from responding to an old memory rather than the situation actually in front of you. Seeing that changes what you do next.

The aim is not to stop feeling.
It's to understand what the feeling is for.
